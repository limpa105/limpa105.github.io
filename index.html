About

Hello! I am a PhD candidate at Stanford University advised by Professor Clark Barrett and working on verifying cryptographic systems with SMT solvers.

Previously I was at University of California, San Diego where I worked with Professor Nadia Polikarpova and Professor Taylor Berg-Kikpatrick on regular expression synthesis from positive examples. I also worked with Professor Michael Coblenz on haskell debugging and scientific computing.

Research Interests

My research interests lie in the intersection of Formal Methods (FM), Cryptography, and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). By combining FM and HCI techniques, I aim to develop intuitive and accessible verification tools for cryptographic systems that maintain provable safety guarantees. I am currently working on cvc5.
